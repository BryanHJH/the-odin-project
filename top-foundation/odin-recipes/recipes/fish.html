Marinated Fried Fish Recipe

Description

Average Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Average Cooking Time: 10 minutes

Additioinal Time: 2 hours

Total time taken: 2 hours 25 minutes

Serving size: 2 pax

Yield: 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 fillets flounder
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons chopped garlic
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon dried dill weed
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed dried rosemary
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 cup vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Place flounder filleets in a small glass dish. Mix lemon juice, garlic, cumin, and paprika in a small bowl; pour over flounder fillets. Cover dish with plastic wrap and marinate flounder in regrigerator for 2 hours.

  2. Mix flour, dill weed, and cayenne pepper together on a piece of waxed paper.

  3. Beat egg and water together in a wide bowl.

  4. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat.

  5. Gently press the flounder fillets into the flour mixture to coat; shake to remove excess flour. Dip into the beaten egg to coat and immediately return to the flour mixture to coat.

  6. Fry flounder in hot oil until the fish flakes easily with a fork, about 5 minutes per side.
